# The Longest Wake — scenes

The spine, not a rail. Players will go sideways; extend in the direction the adventure points (contracts, hospitality, the letter versus the spirit). The clock that matters: **the stores run out tomorrow, and tonight is the ninth night — the Neighbors' clerk closes the ledger at the ninth dawn** (see `secrets.md`).

## 1. The ford at dusk

Approach: rich land, fat cattle, new thatch — and a queue of strangers walking *into* the village while a thinner, better-fed queue strolls out one gate to re-enter by the other. From the great roundhouse: harp, laughter, and a smell of stew that is mostly water by now.

Guest-right is offered at the gate — it must be, and a sharp eye (DC 12 Wisdom (Insight)) reads the cost of it in the greeter's face. The party is fed, seated, and inside the situation within the hour.

**Clue on the threshold:** the dew on the path from the barrow, at dawn, holds a line of bare footprints — walking *toward* the roundhouse — that do not bend the grass. Lugán the quarry lad will show anyone who asks, for the price of being taken seriously.

## 2. The wake

The set-piece social scene; run it as long as it is fun. Old Mórr sits in his chair at the head of the fire, dead, dignified, damp at the collar, smelling faintly of hawthorn blossom — out of season. The room behaves around him with the specific etiquette of people who have had nine days to normalize the impossible.

Running elements, in any order:

- **Fial** (acting chieftain) publicly magnificent, privately drowning; she will draw capable-looking guests aside — see her entry in `npcs.md` for what she asks and what she omits.
- **Nes** sings. When asked for "Mórr's Luck" — the song everyone requests — she performs a *shortened* version. A bard, or DC 13 Intelligence (History) / any West training, notices verses are missing. The missing verses are the receipt (`secrets.md` §The song).
- **Bré**, ninth-day guest, eats. Converses beautifully. Never says anything. (Insight DC 16: his grief is *technically perfect* — performed to the letter, nothing underneath.) Do not out him cheaply; see `npcs.md`.
- **Étar** the herb-wife, who has prepared the body for burial *five times*, will talk shop to anyone respectful: he is not decaying ("he's very considerate that way"), the damp is river-water though the barrow is dry, and once — she'll only say this outside, quietly — at midnight she heard him whisper. One word. *"The stave."*
- **Midnight, on cue:** if the party is present at midnight, Mórr's lips move. Same word. It is not addressed to anyone. It is addressed to the room, the way a man repeats the one thing he needs remembered.

**The pressure:** during the evening, the food visibly thins. If the party does nothing by scene's end, Fial swallows her pride and asks outright — bury my father so this ends, and the Ford will owe you what it can still pay.

## 3. Threads to pull (any order, player-led)

### The barrow
Every rite was done rightly — that is confirmable and true; the failure is not procedural. In the barrow (DC 13 Intelligence (Investigation)): the stone bed holds a *shape* of river-damp where a dry corpse should have lain, and hawthorn petals. From the barrow mouth, sightline to a lone hawthorn on a green mound upstream — the hollow hill (scene 5). Nobody local will look directly at it.

### The song
Nes, pressed kindly (DC 12 Charisma (Persuasion), automatic if a PC performs at the wake first and does it honor), admits the missing verses and sings them privately. They tell, in praise-song sideways-speak: forty years ago, young Mórr walked up at dusk to "the green door" and came down at dawn with luck in both hands, and the price is named only as *"a night of the finest voice at his fire, payable when he leaves it."* Nes has worked out what everyone else refuses to: **she** is the finest voice at Mórr's fire. She is calm about it in the way of someone who has already decided (see `npcs.md`).

### The bog
Étar's whisper plus the song point the same way: the terms exist, written down the only way this world writes — an ogham-cut **tally stave**, which Mórr gave to the bog with words, forty years ago, so it would *stay put* (the bog remembers; see the world doc). Lugán knows which pool the old men avoid.

**Set piece — the bog at night.** Sucking peat (difficult terrain; a fall risks DC 12 Strength save or begin sinking — a sinking creature is restrained, DC 12 Athletics to pull free or be freed). Two **will-o'-wisps** (SRD stat block) rise when the stave's pool is disturbed — the bog's words defend what it keeps. They fight to drive off, not kill, and wink out the moment the stave leaves the water — the bog keeps things only until asked *correctly*: the stave was given "until Mórr's people need the truth of it," and a party retrieving it *for the village* satisfies the letter. A party grabbing it for leverage or loot does not — for them the wisps fight in earnest.
*(Scaling: one wisp for a bruised party; add "the pool's cold grip" — DC 12 Constitution save at round start within the pool or 1 level of the chilled effect: −10 ft speed, improvised and marked as such at the table.)*

**The stave** (plot item, `loot.md`): ogham-style notches any druid, bard, or West-trained reader can sound out. The full terms, letter-exact — see `secrets.md` §The bargain. Things taken from the bog bring their words with them: from the moment it surfaces, everyone touching it *knows* the terms in their bones, which saves a lecture.

## 4. The ninth dawn (timed turn)

At dawn after the ninth night, Bré rises, thanks Fial for a hospitality "kept to the letter," and walks toward the hollow hill without hurrying. The ledger is closing: as of the ninth dawn, the Neighbors deem the debtor's fire *abandoned by its finest voice* unless payment or renegotiation has begun — and their idea of enforcement escalates from returning the collateral to **collecting Nes** (she will walk out to meet them; she volunteered in her head days ago). The party has until Bré reaches the hill to act, negotiate, or interfere. Interfering with Bré directly is possible and unwise (he does not fight; he *notes things down*).

## 5. The hollow hill

A green mound, a lone hawthorn, a door that is only a door at dusk and dawn. Inside: one room that is much larger than the mound, a fire that gives light but no heat, and **the Gentleman of the Hill** (`npcs.md`) — tall, wintry, courteous, with Bré at his shoulder holding the ledger.

This is the climax and it is a **negotiation**, run strictly by Neighbor physics (world doc): they cannot lie, they keep the letter, they honor exact language, and they find mortals interesting. Live options, all of which work if played to the letter:

- **Pay the debt as written.** A night of song from the finest voice at Mórr's fire. If Nes sings it: she is released at "dawn" — the classic risk is *whose* dawn; a sharp party pins the contract to "the next dawn at Fiacc's Ford" (the Gentleman smiles; well negotiated). If a **PC bard performs at the wake first and is publicly acclaimed there**, the PC is arguably now the finest voice at that fire — the letter permits substitution, and the Gentleman will accept a PC's night of song with real interest. Inside the hill, a night of song is a genuine trial: three performances (any mix of Charisma (Performance) DC 13/15/17 — instruments, voice, or story all count); failing one costs the singer a memory of their choice (marked improvised; agree it at the table), failing all three means the debt stands unpaid.
- **Renegotiate.** The Gentleman is *bored of the corpse arrangement* — returning a dead man nightly is beneath his house's dignity, and he'll say so in eleven words or fewer. A new clause that ends embarrassment on both sides (e.g., prosperity ends cleanly, debt forgiven; or a smaller recurring courtesy — first milk of spring left at the hill) is genuinely available to a party that proposes exact wording. Sloppy wording gets accepted *as spoken*, which is its own lesson.
- **The sky-iron option.** Fial's house holds an heirloom sky-iron knife (`loot.md`). Drawn inside the hill it is a declaration: the Gentleman will not touch the bearer, the debt is voided by force majeure — and so is the prosperity, all of it, and the Heron People acquire a Neighbor grudge with no expiry date. The village knows this in its bones; if the party chooses it anyway, it *works*, and the epilogue is colder.
- **Walk away.** The wake never ends. The Ford eats itself hollow within the season. The Gentleman finds this acceptable; the ledger does not care.

**If it comes to blows** (it shouldn't, but players): the Gentleman of the Hill — improvised stat block in `npcs.md`, marked as such — does not fight to kill; he fights to *conclude*, and concluding may include the door not being where it was.

## 6. Epilogue

Settled well: Mórr stays buried at the next attempt — the barrow takes him like a held breath released — the wake ends with one last, real lament, and Fial pays what was promised and names the party guest-friends of the Ford forever (worth more than the coin, and she knows it). Settled by iron or not at all: play the cost honestly.

However it ends, someone at the fire asks Nes for "Mórr's Luck," and she sings — for the first or last time — *all* the verses.
