# The Longest Wake — NPCs

Each entry: want · voice · use. Stat blocks are SRD by reference or marked improvised (CLAUDE.md hard rule 4).

## Old Mórr — the guest of honor
Dead. Present. Chieftain of Fiacc's Ford for forty years, sharp trader, beloved tyrant, the kind of man who read every agreement twice and signed the important one anyway. **Wants** (yes, still): the stave found — he cannot rest with the terms unspoken; his whisper at midnight ("*the stave*") is the only motion left in him. **Voice:** none, barring that word. **Use:** furniture with gravity. The players should end the night fond of a corpse.
*Stats: none. He is beyond hit points.*

## Fial — acting chieftain, his daughter
Runs the Ford, the wake, and her own grief with the same iron competence, and is one honest sentence from breaking. **Wants:** her father buried, the guests gone, and *no one to ever know how close the stores are to empty* — she will lie about the food before she lies about anything. **Voice:** short declaratives; hospitality as armor ("You'll eat first. Then we'll talk."). **Use:** quest-giver, moral anchor, and the cost of failure made visible. She does not know about the bargain — she was born two years after it — but she knows her father feared hawthorn and would never say why.
*Stats: SRD **noble** if it matters; it shouldn't.*

## Nes — the bard, his fosterling
Young, West-trained, the finest voice on that stretch of river, and the only person in the village who has done the arithmetic. **Wants:** to pay the debt herself before anyone else is taken — she has already decided, which is different from being at peace. **Voice:** everything true is said in song-quotes and deflection; direct questions get performed answers. **Use:** the emotional spine. The party can save her, substitute for her, or let her go; the adventure keeps score.
*Stats: SRD **acolyte** reskinned — swap divine flavor for bardic (marked improvised).*

## Étar — the herb-wife
Has prepared Old Mórr for burial five times and taken it as a professional insult approximately zero times; she likes him better than most living clients. **Wants:** to be believed about the details (the damp, the hawthorn, the whisper). **Voice:** clinical warmth; gallows humor with clean fingernails ("He's very considerate that way."). **Use:** clue dispenser who makes the impossible feel documented.
*Stats: SRD **commoner**.*

## Lugán — the quarry lad
Fifteen, employed hauling quartz for the Mound, first to see the dawn footprints, tired of being fifteen every time he reports something important. **Wants:** to be taken seriously by exactly one adult. **Voice:** fast, precise, braced for dismissal. **Use:** free exposition for any party that treats him well; he knows the paths, the bog pools, and which mound nobody looks at.
*Stats: SRD **commoner**.*

## Bré — the ninth-day guest
A flawless guest: eats well, grieves beautifully, helps with nothing, and has restarted his three-night right three times with the punctuality of a man keeping books. Because he is one. **Bré is the Neighbors' clerk**, attending in mortal shape to verify contract compliance until the ninth dawn. He cannot lie; he has simply never needed to volunteer anything. If confronted with proof (the stave, the missing verses sung to his face), he corrects nothing, confirms the letter of whatever is put to him exactly, and compliments the party's diligence. **Wants:** the ledger closed cleanly, either way. **Voice:** perfect manners with no fingerprints on them. **Use:** the players' first Neighbor — unfailingly polite, quietly wrong in the way winter is.
*Stats: improvised (marked): SRD **spy** chassis; plus at will — becomes unnoticed when no one is directly watching (as invisibility, ends if he acts); immune to charm and sleep; sky-iron burns him. He never fights; he leaves.*

## The Gentleman of the Hill
The Neighbor who made the bargain, forty years into an arrangement he now finds undignified. Tall as a spear, beautiful the way winter is beautiful, courteous to the last letter. **Wants:** the account settled without embarrassment to his house — he is *bored* of returning a corpse nightly, and boredom in his kind is nearly a mortal weakness; a party offering an elegant exit negotiates uphill-with-the-wind. **Voice:** never lies, never hurries, answers exactly the question asked and not one word more. **Use:** the climax. Run him by the world doc's Neighbor physics; let exact language win and sloppy language teach.
*Stats: improvised (marked), only if players force it: SRD **knight** chassis, AC 17, 60 HP; his blade is winter (as longsword, damage is cold); 3/day — step from any shadow to any other within sight (as misty step); 1/day — a spoken word of binding (as hold person, DC 14); immune to charm, fright, sleep; **sky-iron negates his damage resistance (bludgeoning/piercing/slashing from nonmagical attacks) and its wounds cannot be regenerated**. He fights to conclude, not to kill; the door participates.*
