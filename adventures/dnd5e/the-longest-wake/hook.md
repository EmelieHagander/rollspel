# The Longest Wake

_A one-shot for four characters of 3rd level. Tone: warm, wet, quietly absurd, and sadder than it first appears. Expected length: one evening._

**GM eyes only — the whole folder. Players learn everything by playing.**

## The situation

Fiacc's Ford is a prosperous village of the Heron People, on a bend of the Whitecow a half-day's walk downstream of the Mound — close enough that its barley and salmon feed the builders, and its luck is locally famous. Old Mórr, its chieftain for forty years, died nine nights ago in his chair by the fire, at a great age, mid-argument, of nothing in particular.

The wake began that night, as is proper: drink, music, competitive grief, guests arriving with the weather. It has not ended, because the funeral keeps failing. Three times now Old Mórr has been laid in the barrow above the village with every rite done rightly — and each following dawn he is back in his chair at the fire. Seated. Composed. Slightly damp. Patiently, politely, and unarguably dead.

Nobody has said the word *Neighbors* aloud yet, because saying it aloud is an invitation, but everyone is thinking it in italics.

Meanwhile the wake, legally, continues — a dead chieftain above ground means the feast is still on, and **hospitality is absolute law**. Word of the marvel has spread up and down the river. Guests keep arriving. Some guests have discovered that walking out one gate and in through the other restarts their three sacred nights. The village that could feed the Mound's builders is nine days into feeding half the valley, the stores are nearly out, and the acting chieftain would rather die than admit it — because admitting the Ford cannot feed its guests is the end of its name forever.

## Why the party is here

Pick whichever fits, or take several:

- **Sent from the Mound.** The Ford's barley shipment is late, and the seer-lines want to know why their supply line has stopped to hold a party. (Ties nicely to any character with West or Center connections.)
- **Guests of the road.** The party claims guest-right for the night — and is welcomed with a warmth that has visible cracks in it. By law they are now *at the wake*.
- **Summoned quietly.** Fial, the acting chieftain, has sent for capable outsiders — outsiders can be asked to do things kin cannot, and paid to forget them.
- **A bard's errand.** Any bard or West-trained character has heard "Mórr's Luck" sung — the old praise-song about the Ford's fortune — and knows that praise-songs that specific usually have a receipt attached.

## What the players should feel in the first hour

A genuinely funny situation — the corpse at his own wake, the professional grievers pacing themselves, the guest on his ninth day of a three-day welcome — resting on something old and cold underneath. The comedy is the crust on the bog. When they push on any detail (the dampness, the hawthorn smell, the prints in the dew), the temperature drops.
