# The Longest Wake — loot

What can be carried away, and what it costs. Provenance per hard rule 4: SRD, or marked improvised.

## The tally stave — plot item
Ogham-cut yew, forty years in the bog, terms legible to any druid/bard/West-trained reader. Not treasure — evidence. Things taken from the bog carry their words with them: whoever holds it knows the terms by heart. After settlement, the Ford will want it burned at the fire it names; a party that asks to keep it instead gets it (Fial is glad to see it gone), and owns a conversation piece that makes druids leave the room. *(Improvised item; no mechanics.)*

## The sky-iron knife — Fial's house heirloom
A dagger of meteoric iron, five generations in the family, kept wrapped in wool and never discussed. **Dagger +1; against creatures of the hollow hills it negates their nonmagical damage resistance and leaves wounds they cannot regenerate** *(improvised properties, marked — sky-iron rules per the world doc)*. Fial will lend it only if the party is going into the hill, with the words "bring it back unused." Given — not lent — only in an ending where the party has saved the Ford at real cost to themselves. If it is *used* in the hill, see `scenes.md` §5: it works, and it costs the village everything the bargain gave.

## Fial's payment — the promised reward
For ending the wake with the Ford's name intact: **25 gp equivalent in this age's wealth** — a mixed pouch (by the vault's five coins: 200 cp, 90 sp, 10 ep, 12 gp, 1 pp — the platinum piece is river-found, drilled, and worn as a bead; Fial calls it "the moon-coin" and parts with it hardest), plus provisions, a night's honor-feast when there is food again, and **guest-friendship of Fiacc's Ford, forever** — named at the fire before witnesses. The guest-friendship is deliberately the item players undervalue tonight and lean on for the rest of the campaign; in this world it is armor made of law.

## The Gentleman's favor — negotiated ending only
If the party settles the account with genuinely elegant exact language (GM's judgment — it should feel *earned*), the Gentleman offers each negotiator a parting courtesy, letter-precise: **"One dawn, when you ask it, weather to your liking."** One use each, any weather for one dawn-to-dusk, asked aloud at dawn. *(Improvised boon, marked. It is exactly what it says — no more, and the players should reread their own request twice before spending it.)* Refusing the favor politely is also safe; the Gentleman notes the wisdom approvingly.

## A PC bard's acclaim — substitution ending only
A PC who sang the night of song in the hill and walked out at the right dawn leaves with **the memory-price waived retroactively** (any memory paid during failed performances is returned at the door — the Gentleman does not keep small change) and a standing, never-stated professional respect from the Neighbors that future adventures may quietly honor. *(Narrative boon, improvised.)*

## Bog-butter
A wooden firkin of it, offered to the party by Étar as travel provisions with complete sincerity. It is butter. It has been in a bog. Both facts are load-bearing. *(SRD-adjacent dairy; no mechanics; morale effects at the table are the players' own problem.)*
