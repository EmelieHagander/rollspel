# SRD quick-reference: conditions

Source: D&D 5e SRD 5.1. This file summarizes rules-as-written; it contains no house rules (those live in `rules/dnd5e/house-rules.md`).

A condition lasts until countered (or per its cause's duration). Multiple instances of the same condition don't stack — the effects apply once.

## Blinded

- Can't see; automatically fails any check requiring sight.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**; its attack rolls have **disadvantage**.

## Charmed

- Can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful abilities/effects.
- The charmer has **advantage** on social ability checks against it.

## Deafened

- Can't hear; automatically fails any check requiring hearing.

## Frightened

- **Disadvantage** on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is in line of sight.
- Can't willingly move **closer** to the source.

## Grappled

- Speed 0; no bonus from any speed increase.
- Ends if the grappler is incapacitated, or if the target is moved out of the grappler's reach by an effect.

## Incapacitated

- Can't take actions or reactions.

## Invisible

- Can't be seen without special means; counts as heavily obscured for hiding. Location can be noticed by noise/tracks.
- Attack rolls against it have **disadvantage**; its attack rolls have **advantage**.

## Paralyzed

- Incapacitated (no actions/reactions), can't move or speak.
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saves.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**; any hit from within 5 ft is a **critical hit**.

## Petrified

- Transformed (with worn/carried nonmagical objects) to inanimate substance; weight ×10; ceases aging.
- Incapacitated, can't move or speak, unaware of surroundings.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**; automatically fails Str and Dex saves.
- **Resistance to all damage**; immune to poison and disease (existing ones are suspended, not cured).

## Poisoned

- **Disadvantage** on attack rolls and ability checks.

## Prone

- Only crawling movement (or stand up: costs half speed).
- **Disadvantage** on its attack rolls.
- Attacks against it: **advantage** if attacker within 5 ft, **disadvantage** otherwise.

## Restrained

- Speed 0; no bonus from any speed increase.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**; its attack rolls have **disadvantage**.
- **Disadvantage** on Dexterity saving throws.

## Stunned

- Incapacitated (no actions/reactions), can't move, speaks only falteringly.
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saves.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**.

## Unconscious

- Incapacitated, can't move or speak, unaware of surroundings.
- Drops what it's holding and falls **prone**.
- Automatically fails Str and Dex saves.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**; any hit from within 5 ft is a **critical hit**.

## Exhaustion

Cumulative levels; effects of the current level include all lower levels. A long rest with food and drink removes one level.

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Disadvantage on ability checks |
| 2 | Speed halved |
| 3 | Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws |
| 4 | Hit point maximum halved |
| 5 | Speed reduced to 0 |
| 6 | Death |
