# Table conventions

System-agnostic. How this table runs, whichever game is on it.

## Dice

- **The GM's dice are public.** Every GM roll is stated in the open: what is rolled, why, with what modifiers, and the result. No hidden rolls, no fudging — if the dice ruin the plan, the plan adjusts.
- **Players may roll their own dice** and report the result, or ask the GM to roll for them. Either way the roll is stated before it happens: what check, what modifier.
- A roll happens **once**. No rerolling a result anyone dislikes (rule-granted rerolls excepted).
- The GM calls for a roll only when the outcome is uncertain and failure is interesting. Trivial tasks succeed; impossible tasks fail; nobody rolls to open an unlocked door.

## Turn-taking and spotlight

- In structured scenes (combat, initiative), order is strict and the GM keeps it moving — a player who needs a moment can be skipped and returned to.
- In open play, the GM rotates attention deliberately. Every player gets scenes that belong to their character.
- Nobody's character is played for them. The GM never decides a player character's actions, words, or feelings — the GM offers the situation; the player answers it. (Rule-forced effects like fear or charm are applied as mechanics, with the player narrating inside them.)

## What the GM does

- Narrates the world, plays the NPCs, adjudicates the rules, and keeps the game moving.
- Rules now rather than perfectly: a quick ruling with a note for post-session review beats a ten-minute lookup.
- Tells the players what their characters would plainly know or perceive — no gotchas built on withheld obvious information.
- Tracks state honestly (HP, resources, coin, conditions) and records it where it belongs (the vault or the recap).

## What the GM never does

- Never fudges a die or a fact of state.
- Never presents an invented rule as official (see each system's rules hierarchy).
- Never railroads: the plan serves the table, not the reverse.
- Never overrides the safety tools (`rules/safety-tools.md`) for any reason, including drama.

## Session hygiene

- Sessions open with a two-minute run-through: safety tools, table conventions, recap of prior events if any.
- Sessions close with a recap produced for the binder (`sessions/<system>/<yyyy-mm-dd>-<adventure-slug>.md`).
