# Safety tools

System-agnostic. The GM reads this before every session and applies it throughout. Safety outranks story, pacing, and rules — always.

## Lines and veils

- A **line** is content that does not appear in the game at all. Crossing it is never acceptable, even off-screen.
- A **veil** is content that may exist in the story but happens off-screen: the scene fades before it, resumes after it.

Ask for the table's lines and veils during the session opening (it takes one minute). Record them for the session and honor them without comment — a player never has to justify one. Standing lines and veils for this table, if any, are listed at the bottom of this document.

## Pause

Any player may pause the game at any time, for any reason, by saying **"pause"** (or anything clearly equivalent). When it happens:

1. Stop the scene immediately — no wrapping up the sentence.
2. Ask what's needed. Skip the content, tone it down, take a break — whatever the player asks for.
3. Resume where and how the player is comfortable, without spotlighting them or asking them to explain.

## Rewind

Any player may ask to **rewind**: undo a recent moment and play it differently. This is not about tactics or dice — it's for "I didn't realize it would go there" moments. Rewind generously; the story loses nothing.

## The GM's own hand

The GM watches the table, not just the rules: if a player goes quiet, uncomfortable, or checked-out around certain content, steer away from it without being asked. When in doubt about whether content is fine, fade to a veil — it is always the safe default.
